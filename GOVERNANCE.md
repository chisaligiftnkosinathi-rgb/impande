# Governance

## Project Purpose
iPhande serves as the community demonstrator for the Computational Continuity Framework (CCF) and the ChemIS interdisciplinary science. It provides the software infrastructure for community-level memory preservation, family heritage, local economic visibility, and continuity.

## Intellectual Property & Stewardship
The intellectual property is distinctly structured:

* **Research Frameworks:** The theoretical underpinnings, including ChemIS, the Computational Continuity Framework (CCF), and the Evidence Passport Specification, are authored and architected by Gift Nkosinathi Chisali.
* **Software Implementations:** Commercial software stewardship, SaaS deployment, and enterprise licensing for the iPhande platforms are managed by Global IT and Business Solutions (Pty) Ltd. Community engagement and social impact are guided by Imbally (Pty) Ltd.

## Collaboration Process
We welcome structured engagement from academic institutions, municipalities, and community organizations.

* **Community Pilot Programs:** Directed to Imbally (Pty) Ltd for local economic development, family heritage, and training.
* **Research Collaboration:** Directed to the Lead Researcher for framework expansion, theoretical validation, or scientific co-publication.
* **Technology Transfer:** Directed to the Commercial Software Steward for institutional integrations.

## Release Process
Releases are versioned strictly. Demonstrators represent continuous integration testing environments but are stabilized via formal release tags (e.g., `v1.0.0-institutional`) prior to external review.
