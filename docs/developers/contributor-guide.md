# Impande Contributor Guide

Thank you for contributing to the preservation of history! Whether you are a family member sharing an old photograph or a researcher transcribing a document, your work ensures that this legacy endures.

## 1. Submitting Evidence
The golden rule of Impande is: **If you know a fact, you must tell us how you know it.**
When submitting a `JournalEntry` (a claim of historical fact), you must attach a `SourceReference`. Even if the source is simply "Oral history passed down from my grandmother," that context is vital for future generations to understand the claim's origin.

## 2. Respecting the Review Process
All submissions are reviewed by Collection Stewards. They may accept your claim, or they may respond and ask for further clarification or documentation. This is not a judgment on your memory; it is the platform's mechanism for ensuring high-confidence historical preservation.

## 3. Disagreeing with Existing History
If you see a fact in the archive that you believe is incorrect, **do not attempt to delete it.** Instead, submit a new `JournalEntry` with your contradictory evidence. The platform is designed to safely hold conflicting histories side-by-side, allowing Stewards to weigh the confidence of both sources transparently.
