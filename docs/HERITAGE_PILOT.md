# The Impande Heritage Pilot Guide

Before a Continuity Space is considered a **Living Archive**, it must be grounded in the lived experience of preserving a real community's history. The Heritage Pilot is a guided milestone to achieve exactly that. 

This guide bridges the gap between the technical Impande Standard and the sacred, practical work of a Steward in the field.

---

## 1. How to Interview an Elder Respectfully

An interview is not an interrogation. It is an act of hospitality.
- **Listen first, record second:** Sit with the elder. Share a meal or a cup of tea. Explain *why* you are preserving this history before you open the application.
- **Let the elder guide the path:** If they wander from the timeline to tell a story about a neighbor, follow the story. The Wisdom Engine feeds on these exact deviations.
- **Do not correct them:** If they state a date you know is wrong, do not interrupt or correct them. Record their claim faithfully. The Truth Engine allows multiple claims to coexist.

## 2. Handling Conflicting Memories

You will inevitably encounter contradictions. Aunt Sarah says George migrated in 1948. Uncle John insists it was 1952.
- **Do not overwrite.** 
- Create two separate Claims attached to George's Entity.
- Attach the Evidence (Oral testimony of Sarah, Oral testimony of John).
- If the truth cannot be resolved, create an **Open Question** marking the conflict. Future generations may find a document that resolves it.

## 3. Recording Uncertainty

When memory fades, it leaves a shadow. Preserve the shadow.
- Do not invent placeholders (e.g., "Unknown First Name").
- Use the **Preserve an Open Question** ceremony to explicitly state: "We do not know the surname of George's brother. The records were lost."
- Document *why* it is unknown and *where* you have already searched, to spare the next generation from repeating dead ends.

## 4. Obtaining Consent

A living archive must respect the living.
- Ask for permission before preserving voices, photographs, or sensitive stories of living individuals.
- Use the **Steward's Journal (Personal)** to note any restrictions requested by an elder (e.g., "Do not share the story of the feud until after my passing"). Personal journal entries remain private and are not exported into the `.impande` archive.

## 5. Photographing and Digitizing

When adding Visual Evidence to the Memory Engine:
- **Handle with clean hands:** Old photographs and documents are fragile.
- **Scan the back:** Always photograph the back of a physical photograph if there is writing. That writing is Evidence.
- **Store originals safely:** Digitization does not replace the physical artifact. Return it to a safe, dark, dry place.

## 6. The Steward's Journal

Use your Journal constantly during the Pilot.
- **Personal Journal:** For your private thoughts, tasks, and emotional reflections ("I felt overwhelmed interviewing Uncle today"). These remain on your device.
- **Research Journal:** For documenting the *process* of history ("I visited the Barberton cemetery today, but the headstones were illegible. Moving on to church records."). These are eligible for preservation in the archive, as they prove the provenance of your search.

## 7. The Final Milestone

A Heritage Pilot is considered complete when you have successfully:
1. Conducted oral interviews and recorded stories.
2. Preserved elder voices using the audio engine.
3. Attached physical/scanned evidence to claims.
4. Exported the Space into a complete `.impande` archive.
5. Imported the archive and verified its cryptographic checksums.

When these steps are complete, the software has vanished, and you have birthed a **Living Archive**.
