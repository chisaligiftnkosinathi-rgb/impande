# Impande Code of Conduct

The Impande platform is built to preserve legacy. Because history is often deeply personal, emotional, and heavily debated, we require all contributors and stewards to adhere to the following principles of collaboration:

1. **Assume Good Intent:** When another contributor submits evidence that contradicts your understanding of history, assume they are acting in good faith based on the records or memories they possess.
2. **Attack the Evidence, Not the Person:** If you disagree with a claim, challenge the provenance, validity, or context of the *evidence*, never the individual who submitted it.
3. **Respect Oral History:** Oral traditions and lived memories are first-class evidence on this platform. They are evaluated differently than primary birth certificates, but they must never be dismissed as invalid out of hand.
4. **No Erasure:** Do not attempt to use technical permissions or administrative authority to erase uncomfortable, contradictory, or conflicting historical claims. The platform is designed to preserve contradictions transparently.
