# The Root System

*An explanation of Impande.*

## Every tree has two parts.

The part above the ground is what everyone sees:
* The trunk.
* The branches.
* The leaves.
* The fruit.

But none of those can exist without the part below the ground:
**The roots.**

The roots do three things:

1. **They anchor the tree.**
Without roots, the tree falls when storms come. In the same way, a person without knowledge of their family, community, and history can struggle to understand where they belong.

2. **They nourish the tree.**
Roots draw water and nutrients that allow new branches to grow. In human life, our roots include the wisdom, sacrifices, values, and experiences of those who came before us.

3. **They preserve continuity.**
Seasons change. Leaves fall. Branches may break. Yet if the roots remain healthy, the tree can continue to grow. Likewise, generations come and go, but if their stories, relationships, and memories are preserved, humanity continues without forgetting itself.

## Why Impande?

In isiZulu, *Impande* means the root.

The system is called Impande because it is not trying to preserve only the visible parts of life. It exists to preserve the roots that make every life possible.

Those roots include:
* Families.
* Communities.
* Languages.
* Traditions.
* Documents.
* Oral history.
* Acts of service.
* Faith and values where families choose to preserve them.
* Evidence that helps future generations understand how we know what we know.

## The Root System in Software

Every record in Impande asks:
* Who was this person or community?
* How are they connected?
* What happened?
* Who told us?
* What evidence supports this?
* What should be preserved for the next generation?

That is the root system.

The family tree that users see is only the branches.

The real work happens underneath—where relationships, memories, evidence, and stories form the living roots that support future generations.

***

> *"A tree is remembered by its fruit, but it lives because of its roots. Impande exists to preserve those roots so that every generation can grow without forgetting where its life began."*
