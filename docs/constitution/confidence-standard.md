# Impande Confidence Standard

This document details how the Confidence Layer evaluates the strength of historical claims on the Impande platform. 

## 1. The Purpose of Confidence
Confidence is not a judgment on the moral value of a story; it is a mathematical and structural assessment of the evidence supporting a claim. A high confidence score means the claim is deeply supported by verifiable sources; a low confidence score means the claim relies on unverified or contradictory information.

## 2. Confidence Factors
The `ConfidenceService` calculates a `ConfidenceScore` (0.0 to 1.0) based on three primary factors:

### A. Source Quality (Weight: 40%)
- **Primary Sources** (e.g., Birth certificates) yield the highest source confidence.
- **Oral History** yields moderate-to-high confidence depending on the proximity of the speaker to the event.
- **No Source** yields a base score of 0.0 for this factor.

### B. Provenance Completeness (Weight: 30%)
- Does the source have a clear origin?
- Is the chain of custody documented?
- A fully documented chain yields 1.0 for this factor.

### C. Corroboration vs. Contradiction (Weight: 30%)
- **Corroboration:** Are there multiple independent sources confirming the same claim? This increases the score.
- **Contradiction:** Are there other accepted claims in the system that logically conflict with this claim? This decreases the score.

## 3. Transparency
Every narrative generated by the Meaning Layer must expose its underlying confidence score. If a reader clicks on a generated biography, they must be able to see the specific math behind the platform's confidence in that story.
